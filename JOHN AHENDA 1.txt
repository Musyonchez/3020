Catalog Course Description
This course aims to enable students to design a knowledge structure integrated with production
planning, quality control and other subsystems of an industrial organization. The course will also enable
students to apply AI techniques to the problem of acquisition and representation of expert knowledge for
problem solving in the expert’s domain.
Credit: 4.5 Credits
Prerequisite: APT3010
Course Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course, students would be able to:
1. Comprehend the foundational principles and diverse categories of Knowledge-Based Systems
(KBS): Students will be able to define KBS, identify their core components and characteristics,
differentiate between various types such as expert systems, rule-based systems, and case-based
systems, and recognize their applications in real-world scenarios.
2. Master fundamental techniques for representing knowledge in AI systems: Students will gain
proficiency in utilizing logic-based approaches (propositional and first-order logic), semantic
networks, frames, and ontologies to model and structure information for effective reasoning
and problem-solving within KBS.
3. Apply and evaluate different reasoning and inference mechanisms used in KBS: Students will
be able to understand and implement rule-based reasoning (forward and backward chaining),
case-based reasoning, and basic methods for handling uncertainty, enabling them to derive new
knowledge and make informed decisions using KBS.
4. Analyze the architecture, development lifecycle, and tools associated with Expert Systems:
Students will develop a comprehensive understanding of expert systems as a significant type of
KBS, including their components, knowledge acquisition methods, development stages, and the
role of various tools and programming languages in their creation.
5. Utilize and compare various problem-solving and search strategies within the context of KBS:
Students will learn about blind and heuristic search algorithms and their critical role in enabling
KBS to navigate through potential solutions and efficiently address complex problems.
6. Explore advanced and contemporary topics in KBS, including machine learning integration and
semantic web technologies: Students will gain an awareness of how machine learning
techniques can enhance knowledge acquisition and refinement in KBS, and understand the
principles and applications of semantic web technologies in building and utilizing large-scale
knowledge systems.

Teaching Methodology
This course employs a blended, student-centered approach that prioritizes active learning and hands-on practice. Key strategies include:
1. Scaffolded Learning: Concepts are introduced incrementally, starting with foundational syntax and progressing to advanced
applications. Each lesson builds on prior knowledge through guided examples and exercises.
2. Interactive Coding Labs: Students engage in real-time coding exercises during class, with immediate feedback from instructors to
reinforce understanding and troubleshoot errors.
3. Collaborative Learning: Pair programming, group projects, and peer code reviews foster teamwork and expose students to diverse
problem-solving approaches.
4. Project-Based Learning: Capstone assignments and a final project simulate real-world challenges, requiring students to synthesize
concepts, debug code, and present solutions.
5. Flipped Classroom Elements: Pre-class resources (e.g., short videos, readings) introduce theoretical concepts, freeing class time for
interactive problem-solving and discussion.
Assessment & Feedback:
• Formative assessments (quizzes, mini-projects) track progress.
• Summative assessments (capstone assignments, final project) evaluate mastery.
• Differentiated instruction accommodates varying skill levels through optional challenges and tailored support.
This methodology ensures students develop technical proficiency, critical thinking, and confidence to tackle real-world programming tasks.
